# Waite Source Documents

Primary source material for A.E. Waite's cartomancy and Tarot methods. All documents are in the public domain. Provided as a free research resource by [Vizcroft Press](https://vizcroft.com).

---

## Source Documents

### A Manual of Cartomancy and Occult Divination (1909)
Arthur Edward Waite's original published method. Contains the English Method of cartomancy using playing cards, the counting and adjacency rules, and the Significator system. This is the public version of a more complex private method Waite taught within his Order.

- [waite_cartomancy_1909.pdf](waite_cartomancy_1909.pdf)

---

### The Pictorial Key to the Tarot (1911)
Waite's companion volume to the Rider-Waite-Smith deck. Contains the Celtic Cross spread, card meanings, and further discussion of the Tarot's symbolism. Published two years after the *Manual*, this is where the Celtic Cross first appeared in print and began its displacement of the older relational method.

- [pictorial_key_tarot_1911.pdf](pictorial_key_tarot_1911.pdf)

---

### Book T: The Tarot (Mathers, 1888)
The internal Golden Dawn manuscript written by MacGregor Mathers. This is the private method Waite was trained in before he simplified it for public consumption. Contains the full five-operation counting system, the Kabalistic attributions, and the Opening of the Key spread. Circulated in manuscript among initiates; never published during Waite's lifetime.

- [mathers_tarot_1888.txt](mathers_tarot_1888.txt)

---

### The Tarot of the Bohemians (Papus, 1892)
Gérard Encausse's codification of the French occult Tarot tradition that preceded and influenced Waite. Contains the counting methods of Etteila and the Egyptian origin theory. Essential context for understanding what Waite inherited and what he changed.

- [papus_tarot_bohemians_1892.txt](papus_tarot_bohemians_1892.txt)
